# Persona: The Multi-Series Tracker

**Name:** Sofía
**Age:** 34
**Occupation:** Marketing manager
**Primary series:** F1, Formula E, WEC, IndyCar

## About

Sofía follows four series religiously. She got into motorsport through F1 but broadened her interest after watching the Le Mans 24h. She uses a shared household TV, so scheduling conflicts between series are a real problem — she needs to know weeks in advance when two series clash so she can negotiate screen time or set a recording.

## Goals

- See all her series on one unified calendar so clashes are obvious
- Plan ahead: identify busy weekends vs. quiet ones
- Filter down to just her series without seeing NASCAR or MotoGP noise
- Mark events she definitely wants to watch live vs. record

## Pain Points

- WEC and IndyCar have no decent English-language calendar apps
- The FIA and IndyCar websites show different timezone conventions
- Clash detection is entirely manual — she keeps a paper calendar

## How She Uses RaceTrack

- Visits the calendar page at the start of each month to plan the month ahead
- Uses series filter to show only F1, FE, WEC, IndyCar
- Adds all conflicting events to watchlist as a reminder list
- Checks event modal for session times when planning recording schedules

## Design Implications

- **Calendar view matters most** — a monthly grid lets her see clashes at a glance
- Series filter must be persistent (or at least fast to re-apply)
- Multi-day event spans on the calendar grid are important (WEC endurance events)
- Watchlist acts as her "to-watch" list, not just a favorites store
- Dense month view (all series visible simultaneously) is more useful than series-by-series
