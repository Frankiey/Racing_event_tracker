# Persona: The Casual Viewer

**Name:** James
**Age:** 41
**Occupation:** Teacher
**Primary series:** F1 (race only), "whatever's on"

## About

James watches the F1 race most Sundays when he's not busy with family commitments. He's not a die-hard — he doesn't watch practice or qualifying, and he doesn't follow standings obsessively. If it's a boring Verstappen cruise he'll switch off. He occasionally catches a NASCAR superspeedway race or an IndyCar oval if it comes up on TV.

## Goals

- Know whether there's a race this weekend and what time it starts
- Not be overwhelmed with session details he doesn't care about
- Quickly see if something interesting is on in the next few days

## Pain Points

- Too much detail (all sessions listed) makes it hard to find "the race"
- Timezone math is confusing — he just wants "Sunday 3pm"
- He's not going to read about series he doesn't know

## How He Uses RaceTrack

- Checks the dashboard on Thursday/Friday to see "is there a race this weekend?"
- Glances at the countdown hero — that tells him everything he needs
- Doesn't use the watchlist, series pages, or calendar in depth

## Design Implications

- The dashboard hero/countdown is his entire use case
- Race session should be visually prominent on cards — FP1/FP2 are noise to him
- "Next up" highlighting is critical
- Keep the dashboard simple enough that he can extract the key info in 5 seconds
- Don't hide the race time behind a modal for casual glancing
